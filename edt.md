# Ethical Dissonance Theory (EDT)
*A multi‑level framework explaining psychological and institutional drivers of scientific conformity*  
**Ramon Alexander Sosich (2026)**

---

## 1. Overview

**Ethical Dissonance Theory (EDT)** is a multi‑level conceptual framework explaining how psychological, social, and institutional forces interact to produce *scientific conformity*, particularly in contexts where ethical, epistemic, or methodological concerns are present.

EDT proposes that conformity in scientific environments is not reducible to individual cognition or institutional incentives alone. Instead, it emerges from the **interaction** of:

- **Micro‑level psychological mechanisms**  
- **Meso‑level social dynamics**  
- **Macro‑level institutional structures**

The theory explains why scientists often align with prevailing norms, practices, or expectations even when these conflict with personal values, empirical evidence, or ethical standards.

---

## 2. The Three‑Level Architecture of EDT

EDT is built on a structured, multi‑layer model:

---

### 2.1 Micro‑Psychological Level  
*Internal mechanisms shaping individual decision‑making*

This level captures the cognitive and emotional processes that generate ethical tension and motivate conformity:

- **Cognitive dissonance** — conflict between personal values and institutional expectations  
- **Moral disengagement** — rationalising ethically questionable behaviour  
- **Motivated reasoning** — interpreting information to reduce internal conflict  
- **Identity‑protective cognition** — aligning beliefs with group identity to maintain belonging  

**Outcome:**  
Individuals resolve ethical tension by aligning with the dominant norm.

---

### 2.2 Meso‑Social Level  
*Interpersonal and group‑level pressures*

This level captures the social dynamics that reinforce conformity:

- **Norm enforcement**  
- **Status hierarchies**  
- **Reputation management**  
- **Peer conformity**  

**Outcome:**  
Social incentives reward alignment and penalise dissent.

---

### 2.3 Macro‑Institutional Level  
*Structural and systemic influences*

This level captures the organisational and systemic forces that shape behaviour:

- **Incentive structures**  
- **Organisational norms**  
- **Gatekeeping mechanisms**  
- **Structural reinforcement**  

**Outcome:**  
Institutions stabilise and perpetuate conformity over time.

---

## 3. Mechanisms of Ethical Dissonance

EDT identifies several mechanisms through which ethical tension is resolved in favour of conformity:

### 3.1 Norm Internalisation  
Institutional norms become personal beliefs through repeated exposure.

### 3.2 Ethical Displacement  
Responsibility is shifted to systems, supervisors, or “the field”.

### 3.3 Epistemic Deference  
Individuals defer to institutional consensus even when uncertain.

### 3.4 Structural Reinforcement  
Systems reward compliance and penalise dissent, shaping behaviour over time.

---

## 4. Conceptual Flow Diagram (Text‑Based)
Ethical Tension → Micro-Level (Psychological)  →  Meso-Level (Social) →  Macro-Level (Institutional)   →   Conformity Behaviour  →  Reinforcement Loop → Back to Ethical Tension

This loop explains how conformity becomes self‑sustaining.

---

## 5. Theoretical Contributions

EDT contributes to:

- **Psychology** — integrating cognitive dissonance, moral psychology, and identity processes  
- **Ethics** — explaining how ethical tension is resolved in institutional contexts  
- **Meta‑science** — offering a framework for understanding systemic conformity  
- **Organisational behaviour** — linking individual cognition to institutional structures  
- **Sociology of science** — explaining how norms and incentives shape scientific behaviour

## 8. Future Development

EDT is intended as a long-term conceptual research programme and open-science framework. Planned future developments include:

- Publication of a full theoretical monograph expanding and refining the EDT framework.
- Development of domain-specific conceptual papers and micro-papers exploring individual constructs, mechanisms, and applications.
- Submission of focused conceptual derivatives to peer-reviewed scholarly journals.
- Operationalisation of EDT constructs into empirically testable propositions and research questions.
- Development of measurement approaches for ethical dissonance and related constructs.
- Case studies and applied analyses across scientific, organisational, healthcare, and institutional contexts.
- Integration with meta-scientific, psychological, and organisational research literatures.
- Empirical investigation of selected EDT propositions through future honours, postgraduate, or collaborative research where appropriate.
- Encouragement of independent scholarly critique, extension, refinement, and testing of EDT concepts by other researchers.

---

### Development Status

Ethical Dissonance Theory (EDT) is an independent conceptual framework currently under active development. The framework remains theoretical in nature and has not yet undergone peer review or empirical testing. Future revisions may refine, expand, modify, or remove elements of the framework as additional scholarship, critique, and empirical evidence emerge.

Current versions should therefore be interpreted as developmental conceptual work rather than finalized scientific conclusions.
