# Ethical Dissonance Theory (EDT)

## Overview
Ethical Dissonance Theory (EDT) is a multi-level conceptual framework explaining how psychological and institutional forces interact to produce scientific conformity. It integrates cognitive, social, and structural mechanisms to explain why individuals and institutions align with prevailing norms even when those norms conflict with ethical or epistemic standards.

EDT proposes that conformity is not merely a cognitive process, nor solely an institutional one — it is an emergent property of interacting psychological, social, and structural pressures.

---

## Core Components

### **1. Psychological Layer**
Internal mechanisms shaping individual decision-making:
- Cognitive dissonance
- Moral disengagement
- Motivated reasoning
- Identity-protective cognition

### **2. Social Layer**
Interpersonal and group-level pressures:
- Norm enforcement
- Status hierarchies
- Peer conformity
- Reputation management

### **3. Institutional Layer**
Structural and systemic influences:
- Incentive structures
- Organisational norms
- Gatekeeping mechanisms
- Career dependencies

---

## Mechanisms of Ethical Dissonance
EDT identifies several mechanisms through which ethical tension is resolved in favour of conformity:

- **Norm Internalisation:** Adopting institutional norms as personal beliefs
- 
- **Ethical Displacement:** Shifting responsibility to systems or authorities
- 
- **Epistemic Deference:** Prioritising institutional consensus over personal judgement
- 
- **Structural Reinforcement:** Systems rewarding compliance and penalising dissent
